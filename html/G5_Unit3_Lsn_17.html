Kinds of Sentences
Lesson: 17 Simple Tense: Present (pp. 80–83)

The tense of a verb tells when an action happens. A verb in the present tense tellsabout an action that is happening now. A verb must agree with its subject. If the subject is singular or he, she, or it, the verb must be singular. If the subject is plural or I, we, you, or they, the verb must be plural. Follow these rules for forming present-tense verbs:

Add -s to form most present-tense singular verbs.
Add -es if the verb ends in s, ch, sh, x, or z.
If the verb ends in a consonant and y, change the y to i and add -es.
Do not add -s or -es to form the plural form of a verb in the present tense.
Write the present-tense form of the verb in parentheses that correctly completes each sentence.
  • Keiki the concert on TV. (watch)
  • A fifth-grader down to the school bus. (hurry)
  • Mr. Timmons his clothes at the laundromat. (wash)
  • The students their projects on time. (complete)
  • The big balloon just the tops of the trees. (miss)
  • My Dad work around midnight. (finish)
  • Sean the puppy’s head gently. (pat)
  • Sometimes the new baby at night. (cry)
  • A small airplane over the lake. (buzz)
  • Delano the batter for the cake. (mix)
  • I Grandma every few days. (visit)
  • Mr. Liu the cabin empty all winter. (leave)
  • The geese in large flocks. (travel)
  • This sweater your pants. (match)
  • Aunt Chandra every new product at the store. (try)
