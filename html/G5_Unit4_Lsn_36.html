Unit 4

ADJECTIVES, ADVERDS, CONJUNCTIONS, AND PREPOSITIONS

Lesson 36: Prepositions (pp. 160–163)

Words that mean ”no” are negatives. The words no, not, nothing, none, never, no one, nowhere, and nobody are negatives. Constructions with not, such as aren’t, doesn’t, and haven’t, are also negatives. The word not is an adverb. A negative idea is expressed by using one negative word. Do not use a double negative.

Write the word in parentheses that correctly completes each sentence.

  • Ryan hasn’t taken (no, any) piano lessons this year.
  • Kyla wasn’t (never, ever) in the hospital.
  • We had a bushel of apples, but Mrs. Davies didn’t want (any, none).
  • Mr. Diaz won’t let (no one, anyone) use the power tools.
  • No one (wouldn’t, would) have guessed the answer.
  • Some people cannot (never, ever) remember a joke.
  • Why didn’t (nobody, anybody) call me about the schedule change?
  • The customers won’t (never, ever) agree to pay that much.
  • The house isn’t vacant (no, any) longer.
  • Why doesn’t (nobody, anybody) believe me?
  • There is (nothing, anything) wrong with this outline.
  • Dad never adds sugar to his cereal (either, neither).
  • No amount of pushing (couldn’t, could) get us moving.
  • We don’t remember (anything, nothing) about the flood.
  • Didn’t (nobody, anybody) help that poor child in the rain?
