SENTENCES

Lesson 3:
Commands and Exclamations (pp. 16–19)

A command tells someone to do something. A command ends with a period.
An exclamation shows strong feeling. An exclamation ends with an exclamation mark (!).

Add a period (.) to end each command. Add an exclamation mark (!) to end each exclamation. The clue in ( ) will help you.
  • What a surprise my birthday party was (exclamation)
  • Please water the plants next week (command)
  • Turn off the light now (command)
  • Those circus clowns are so funny (exclamation)
  • Watch out for the puddles (command)
  • Clean your room this Saturday (command)
  • Wow, the fireworks are amazing (exclamation)
  • Oh, that rabbit is so cute (exclamation)
  • Wear your heavy coat today (command)
  • Take good care of your new puppy (command)
  • I can’t believe how much money you saved (exclamation)
  • Tell him right away (command)
  • What a beautiful picture that is (exclamation)
  • Make sure you stay on the path (command)
  • This is great news (exclamation)
