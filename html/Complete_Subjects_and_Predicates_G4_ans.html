SENTENCES

Lessson 2: Complete Subjects and Predicates (pp. 12–15)

The subject of a sentence tells whom or what the sentence is about.
The complete subject includes all the words in the subject part of the sentence.
The predicate of a sentence tells what the subject does or is.
The complete predicate includes all the words in the predicate part of the sentence.

Read each sentence. Draw a line between the complete subject and the complete predicate. Underline the complete subject once and the complete predicate twice.

  • The cows|looked at us with curiosity.
  • Danny|checks the spelling of the word.
  • My cat|sleeps about twenty hours a day.
  • The express train|arrives exactly at noon.
  • The baseball team|practices at Memorial Field.
  • Fatimah|pressed the button for the elevator.
  • A large refrigerator|uses a lot of electricity.
  • Crickets|chirped outside our window.
  • The fourth graders|sat in front of the fifth graders.
  • The exciting new invention|was a huge success.
  • We|wait patiently in the long line for tickets.
  • Many people|came to the Fourth of July picnic.
  • Our teacher|writes the vocabulary words on the chalkboard.
  • The audience|applauds.
  • Eric|meets us at the park.
