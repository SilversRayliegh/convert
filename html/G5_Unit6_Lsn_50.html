Lesson 50: Commas in a Series (pp. 224–227)

A comma separates words or ideas in a sentence and tells the reader when to pause. Use commas to separate items in a series.
A series is a list of three or more words or phrases. A conjunction such as and or or is used before the last item. Do not use a comma after the last item.

Find the series in each sentence. Add commas to separate the words or phrases in the series.
  • Donald Duck’s nephews were named Huey, Dewey, and Louie.
  • Bowling, tennis, and jogging are my favorite activities.
  • Chuck stuffed himself with apples, peanut butter, and milk.
  • Karen aimed, threw, and just missed the bull’s eye.
  • We have no glue, tape, or other way to attach the balloons.
  • The volcano was powerful, dangerous, and loud.
  • Sue’s parents, brother, and sister all drove her to camp.
  • Thunder, lightning, and heavy rains spoiled the weekend.
  • Rex jumped through the screen door, raced across the street, and terrified our neighbors.
  • The entire audience cheered, clapped their hands, and roared for more.
  • We ran around the track, played softball, and swam in the pool.
  • Tina campaigned tirelessly, spoke eloquently, and won the election handily.
  • The baby was smiling, gurgling, and clapping his tiny hands.
  • We enjoyed fried rice, a meat dish, egg rolls, and fortune cookies.
  • Somehow Dad lost the map, made a wrong turn, and followed the wrong trail for miles.
