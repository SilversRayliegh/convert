Lesson 31: Comparing with Adjectives (pp. 140–143)

An adjective has different forms for comparing. Use either -er or more to compare two people, places, things, or ideas. Use either -est or most to compare more than two items. When you add -er or -est, the spelling may change. Do not use -er or -est together with more and most.

  • The trunk of the elm tree is than the oak’s. (wide)
  • The art in the lobby was than the art in the gallery. (impressive)
