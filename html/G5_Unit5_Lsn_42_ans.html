Lesson 42: Using I and Me, We and Us (pp. 188–191)

The pronoun I is a subject pronoun. Use I as the subject of a sentence. The pronoun me is an object pronoun. Use me as a direct object or as the object of a preposition. When you speak or write about yourself and another person, always name the other person first. Use the rules above to help you decide which pronoun to use. Sometimes a noun is used with we or us to make clear whom you are talking about. Use we with noun subjects. Use us with a direct object or the object of a preposition.

Write the word or words in parentheses that correctly complete each sentence.
  • My father and I built this boat last summer. (I, me)
  • My grandmother gave me this bicycle. (I, me)
  • We have been playing chess all afternoon. (Us, We)
  • Rex and I can speak Spanish pretty well. (Rex and me, Rex and I)
  • We fifth-graders must work together! (Us, We)
  • My cousin and I visited the museum Sunday. (I, me)
  • The armor impressed Juan and me the most. (me and Juan, Juan and me)
  • Those teenagers just laughed at us kids. (we, us)
  • The audience clapped for the musicians and us . (we, us)
  • Will you tell us children about the new house? (us, we)
  • Most of the time, Uncle Al and I get along well. (Uncle Al and I, I and Uncle Al)
  • No snowstorm ever stopped my mother and me . (me and my mother, my mother and me)
  • After the play, Frank and I took the bus home. (I, me)
  • You have us fire fighters to thank for that! (us, we)
  • We four sisters have always helped each other. (Us, We)
