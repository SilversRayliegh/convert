Unit 4

ADJECTIVES AND ADVERBS

Lesson 33: Negatives (pp. 148–151)

A negative is a word that means “no.” Do not use two negative words together in one sentence.

Write the word in ( ) that correctly completes each sentence.

  • The girl in the story couldn’t find the hidden treasure anywhere. (nowhere, anywhere)
  • I won’t ever ride that way to school. (ever, never)
  • I am not afraid of any ghosts. (no, any)
  • There is no clown in that show. (is, isn’t)
  • I haven’t seen anyone yet. (anyone, nobody)
  • The detective has found no clues yet. (has, hasn’t)
  • We haven’t got a reservation anywhere that night. (anywhere, nowhere)
  • You are not surprised at all! (are, aren’t)
  • We could hear no music in the back row of seats. (could, couldn’t)
  • We didn’t pass anyone on the road. (nobody, anyone)
  • There was no cake left in the pan. (was, wasn’t)
  • The captain can see no boats in the fog. (can, can’t)
  • I have seen no photographs from the party. (have, haven’t)
  • The mayor didn’t say anything to the reporters. (nothing, anything)
  • That doesn’t make a difference now. (no, a)
