Proofreading (Lessons 24-34, pp. 112-155)

          What’s the difference between a seal and a sea lion? How can you tell a octopus from a squid? Our classmates did some research. Here are the most importantest differences they found.

     Seals and sea lions both have round heads, but their necks are not the same. A sea lion’s neck is thickest than a seal’s. Also, both animals hear good, but their ears look different. A sea lion has small ears. A seal’s ears are tinyer than a sea lion’s. In fact, they are so tiny that you can’t see no ears at all.

     Octopuses and squids both have soft bodies, but a squid’s body is much longer and thiner. These here animals also seem alike at first because of their many arms. If you look closely, however, you will see that an octopus has eight arms and an squid has ten.

     Next week, our class will learn how a alligator is different from a crocodile. We will study the animals more closelier than ever. We are good observers. Soon, we will be best observers than we are now.
