Lesson 22: Progressive Forms of Verbs (pp. 100–103)

The progressive form of a verb shows ongoing or continuous action. Progressive verbs can show action in the present, past, or future. To form a progressive verb, join one of the helping verbs was, were, am, is, are, or will be to a main verb.

Write the correct present, past, or future progressive form of the verb in parentheses on the line.
  • The puppy was jumping around, and Maggie (laugh) at it. was laughing
  • Right now I am not sure if I (go) to the magic show. am going
  • Next week he (play) a concert in the park. will be playing
  • The actors (perform) this play very well. are performing
  • The doorknob broke, but Dad (fix) it now. is fixing
  • She (bake) a cake when she ran out of sugar. was baking
  • Next month, the athlete (run) in a long race. will be running
  • He (drink) ice-cold lemonade because it was a hot day. was drinking
  • I (call) my brother, but he is not answering. am calling
  • When you (sing), everyone was enjoying your song. were singing
  • It is not polite to interrupt someone who (talk). is talking
  • Wait until the mural we (paint) is done!are painting
  • Now I (look) for shells to decorate my sandcastle. am looking
  • They (ride) the bus when a traffic jam happened. were riding
  • Do you know what we (eat) for dinner tonight? will be eating
