Lesson 48: Abbreviations (pp. 216–219)

An abbreviation is a short form of a word that begins with a capital letter and ends with a period. Use most abbreviations only in special kinds of writing, such as addresses. An initial is an abbreviation of a first or middle name. An initial is written as a capital letter followed by a period. State names used with ZIP codes have two-letter abbreviations. Capitalize both letters, but don’t use periods
