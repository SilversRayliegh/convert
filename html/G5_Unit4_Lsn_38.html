Lessson 38: Prepositions (pp. 168–171)

A preposition is a word that relates a noun or pronoun to some other word in the sentence. The object of the preposition is the noun or pronoun that follows a preposition.

Underline the preposition in each sentence. Then write the object of the preposition on the line.

  • The library will hold the book until tomorrow.
  • A small plane had landed in the cornfield.
  • During the night, the thunder awakened us.
  • The bicycle shop on Green Bay Road does good work.
  • Cross-country skiing through the forest is always fun.
  • The pancake house near the expressway is always open.
  • Marsha and Jory went biking along the lakeshore.
  • Cory mixed the powder with the paint.
  • From the top step, the old cat watched everything.
  • The woman in the white suit is our lawyer.
  • Behind home plate is the batting cage.
  • The sailboat is drifting toward the sandbars.
  • Under the bridge, the river flowed quite swiftly.
  • Coach Herman rushed onto the field.
  • Alana sat beside the fireplace and played her mandolin.
