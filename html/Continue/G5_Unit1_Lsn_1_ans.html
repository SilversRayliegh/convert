SENTENCES

Lesson 1: Kinds of Sentences (pp. 8–11)

A sentence is a group of words that expresses a complete thought.
A declarative sentence makes a statement. It ends with a period (.).
An interrogative sentence asks a question. It ends with a question mark (?).
An imperative sentence gives a command or makes a request. It ends with a period (.).
An exclamatory sentence shows strong feelings. It ends with an exclamation point (!).

Add the correct end punctuation to each sentence. Then write declarative, interrogative, imperative, or exclamatory to tell what kind of sentence it is.

  • Look at that strange mask .
  • Please be careful over there .
  • Where were peanuts first grown ?
  • Jana worked on her report in the library .
  • What a weird noise that is !
  • Our team made three touchdowns .
  • Did Julio really score three goals in the game ?
  • Several sparrows visit our birdfeeder every morning .
  • This movie is really scary !
  • Ask the librarian for books about earthquakes .
  • My Aunt Xiang makes pottery .
  • Have you visited the Willis Tower on Wacker Drive ?
  • Delores, please come to the movie with me .
  • Hey, these chicken wings taste great !
  • Is your family really moving to California next month ?
