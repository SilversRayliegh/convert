Nouns

Lesson 23: Contractions with Not (pp. 104–107)

A contraction is made by joining two words together. An apostrophe (’) takes the place of any letters that are left out. Some contractions are formed by joining a verb with the word not.

Write a contraction for the pair of words in parentheses to complete each sentence.

  • James bring his lunch. (did not)
  • My little brother afraid of the dark. (is not)
  • Whales fish. (are not)
  • The dog sit still. (would not)
  • Bumblebees sting people. (do not)
  • Those sneakers the right size for me. (were not)
  • The snowstorm started yet. (has not)
  • Marissa ever flown on a plane before. (had not)
  • We read the faded handwriting. (could not)
  • I stay on the phone too long. (should not)
  • Ostriches climb trees. (can not)
  • The baby sleepy. (was not)
  • Our guests arrived yet. (have not)
  • That electric fan make any noise. (does not)
  • Mom let us eat ice cream before dinner. (will not)
