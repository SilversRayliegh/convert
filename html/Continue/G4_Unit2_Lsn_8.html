Lesson 8: Common and Proper Nouns (pp. 40–43)

A noun is a word that names a person, place, thing, or idea. An idea can be expressed by an abstract noun such as freedom or adulthood. A common noun names any person, place, or thing. A proper noun names a specific person, place, or thing. It can be one word or more than one word. Each important word in a proper noun begins with a capital letter.

Read each sentence. Write common if the noun in boldface is a common noun. Write proper if it is a proper noun.

  • Our class went on a field trip to Miller Pond.
  • Ray needs that magnet for his science project.
  • You can see the Brooklyn Bridge from this window.
  • Dr. Edwards is an eye doctor.
  • Ms. Pineda is a teacher.
  • Are there many volcanoes in Hawaii?
  • Those dolphins live in the Pacific Ocean.
  • The beach will close right after Labor Day.
  • Jason is using the new computer.
  • He is very proud of his intelligence.
  • Does Omar write both books and poems?
  • Each student will report on a famous inventor.
  • The Red Sox are playing a game tomorrow.
  • The poem is about liberty.
  • Are there thirty-one days in April?
