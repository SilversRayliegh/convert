Lesson 13: Present-Tense Verbs (pp. 64–67)

The tense of a verb tells when an action happens. A verb in the present tense tells about an action that happens now or happens often. A present-tense verb must agree with the subject of a sentence. The subject and the verb must both be singular or plural.

Write the present-tense form of the verb in parentheses to correctly complete each sentence.

  • Dad eggs for breakfast. (boil)
  • The magnet the paper clips. (attract)
  • Nasser art out of leaves and grass. (create)
  • The carpenter each board. (measure)
  • Computers huge amounts of information. (store)
  • That watch new batteries. (need)
  • The customers stamps at the post office. (buy)
  • Yellow leaves in the pond. (float)
  • Water at zero degrees Celsius. (freeze)
  • Six white horses the prince’s carriage. (pull)
  • Butterflies sweet liquid from flowers. (sip)
  • The hockey player before the game. (practice)
  • The little brown rabbit its nose. (wiggle)
  • Comets across the sky. (streak)
  • The bookstore very soon. (close)
