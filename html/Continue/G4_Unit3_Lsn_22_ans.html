VERBS

Lesson 22: More Irregular Verbs (pp. 100–103)

Irregular verbs do not form the past tense by adding -ed. Instead, the spelling of an irregular verb changes when the past tense is formed. The spelling may change again when the irregular verb is used with the helping verbs has, have, and had.

Write the past-tense form of the verb in parentheses to correctly complete each sentence.

  • We took the subway to Yankee Stadium. (take)
  • Linda has broken the record for the fastest run. (break)
  • One of the firefighters drove the fire truck. (drive)
  • The butterflies have flown to their winter home in Mexico. (fly)
  • Dad told stories about his childhood. (tell)
  • The crowd sang the national anthem. (sing)
  • The graduating students have thrown their hats in the air. (throw)
  • Li wrote a spooky story. (write)
  • The baker has taken the bread out of the oven. (take)
  • James Howe has written many books for children. (write)
  • The boy threw a stick for his dog. (throw)
  • The zipper on my jacket broke. (break)
  • We rode our bicycles up the steep hill. (ride)
  • The bats flew back to their cave. (fly)
  • Martin drew a border around his picture. (draw)
