Lesson 20: Progressive Forms of Verbs (pp. 92−95)

The present, past, and future verb tenses each have a progressive form. The progressive form shows that an action is ongoing. To form the present progressive, use the helping verb am, is, or are with the –ing form of the main verb. To form the past progressive, use the helping verb was or were with the –ing form of the main verb. To form the future progressive, use the helping verb will be with the –ing form of the main verb.

Read each sentence. The verb in parentheses needs to be changed to its progressive form. Choose a helping verb from the box. Then write the correct present, past, or future progressive form of the verb in parentheses on the line.
