Lesson 7: Correcting Fragments and Run-ons (pp. 32–35)

A fragment is an incomplete sentence. A run-on sentence is two complete sentences that run together.

Make each fragment a complete sentence by matching it with the correct subject or predicate. Write the etter of the words you choose on the line.

  • Pine cones
  • helped me find a book.
  • move tons of dirt.
  • The librarian
  • grow on pine trees.
  • The big bulldozers
Write each run-on sentence as a compound sentence.
  1. Sarah plays baseball she plays basketball, too.
  2. Some kinds of jellyfish can sting most kinds are not dangerous.
  3. Don’t leave the ice cubes in the sun they will melt quickly.
Write each run-on sentence as two separate sentences.
  1. Winter will soon be here the days are getting shorter.
  2. Dad fixed the flat tire I can ride my bicycle again.
  3. The fire drill was over we went back to our classroom.
  4. My grandmother lives next door I see her almost every day.
