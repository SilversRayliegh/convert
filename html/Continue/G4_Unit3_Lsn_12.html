VERBS

Lesson 12: Action Verbs (pp. 60–63)

The verb is the main word in the predicate of a sentence. Often, the verb is a word that shows an action. An action verb tells what the subject does or did.

Draw a line between the subject and the predicate of each sentence. Then write the action verb.

  • The trick-or-treaters  ring the doorbell.
  • The movers  unloaded the truck.
  • Baby turtles  hatch from eggs.
  • A famous author  visited our class.
  • The explorers  plan a new adventure.
  • Beth  added the numbers.
  • The kittens  yawn.
  • The campers  play a tune on their harmonicas.
  • The last two lines of that poem  rhyme.
  • Desert  plants survive for many days without water.
  • Jaguars  climb trees.
  • The barber  cuts the children’s hair.
  • The chipmunks  stuff their cheeks with food.
  • My bicycle helmet  protects my head.
  • The students  planted trees on Earth Day.
