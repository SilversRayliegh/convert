NOUNS

Lesson 10: Common and Proper Nouns (pp. 48–51)

A noun is a word that names a person, place, or thing. Nouns can also name things that you cannot see, such as ideas.
A proper noun names a specific person, place, thing, or idea. Capitalize all the important words in a proper noun.
A noun that does not name a specific person, place, thing, or idea is a common noun.
Common nouns are not capitalized.

In each sentence, underline the proper noun. Then write it correctly on the line.

  • My grandparents live near the hudson river.
  • We stayed in a small hotel on market street.
  • Is sequoia national park a nice place to visit?
  • Last night georgetown won the basketball game.
  • The sailboat crossed the gulf of mexico.
  • Your postcard from peru arrived yesterday.
  • Tell ginny about tomorrow’s brunch downtown.
  • Will mayor dixon speak at today’s assembly?
  • The village of fair oaks is down the road.
  • I told dr. ruiz that I feel better.
  • The picnic at overton park was fun!
  • My brother goes to the university of chicago.
  • Our next meeting will be the last week in october.
  • The maplewood garden club planted the flowers.
  • The class finished the book before memorial day.
