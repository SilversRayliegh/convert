Lesson 45: Contractions with Pronouns (pp. 200–203)

A contraction is a shortened word made by combining two words. An apostrophe (') takes the place of any letters that are left out. Many contractions are formed by combining a pronoun and a verb. The contractions for the pronouns with the verbs is and has are the same. The contractions for the pronouns with the verbs would and had are also the same.

Write a contraction for the pronoun and verb in boldface.
  • We will visit the Jefferson Memorial later today.
  • I have always wanted to try hang-gliding.
  • Mr. Lucas is out of town; he is giving a speech in Akron.
  • I think you will really like these egg rolls.
  • Let’s rest now, because we are going to race later.
  • Aunt Emma met you years ago, but she has never forgotten you.
  • Did you tell Mr. Sable that I am joining
  • The Davises expect us, and they have prepared a few surprises.
  • We would have called earlier if we knew your number.
  • I had been ill with the flu but feel fine now.
  • She has been late for practice too often this month.
  • The hotel is rather old, but it is still comfortable.
  • When the teachers are ready, they will call for you.
  • I have got to cross that river one way or another.
  • The weather has been awful; it has rained every day.
