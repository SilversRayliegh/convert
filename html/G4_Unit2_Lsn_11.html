Lesson 11: Possessive Nouns (pp. 52–55)

A possessive noun is a noun that shows who or what has something. Both singular and plural nouns can be made possessive.

Write the possessive form of the noun in parentheses that correctly completes each sentence.

  • The teacher collected the homework. (students)
  • I wrote address on the envelope. (David)
  • The color helps it hide among the plants. (insect)
  • The hurricane damaged the roofs. (houses)
  • The swim team is training for the Olympics. (women)
  • This cheese is made from milk. (sheep)
  • The office is on the third floor. (doctor)
  • Each player on the volleyball team received a medal. (men)
  • Does this library have a room? (children)
  • dog is very smart. (Malik)
  • The roots grow deep into the ground. (tree)
  • We will read the famous biographies. (scientists)
  • Luis borrowed his camera. (sister)
  • The ice skaters waited for the scores. (judges)
  • A skin can be green or yellow. (banana)
