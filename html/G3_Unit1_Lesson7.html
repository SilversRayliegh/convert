SENTENCES

Lesson 7: Complex Sentences (pp. 32–35)

A complex sentence has two related ideas. The related ideas are joined by a subordinating conjunction. The subordinating conjunction may come in the middle of the sentence or at the beginning of the sentence.

Read each sentence. Circle complex if the sentence is made up of two related ideas joined by a subordinating conjunction. Then underline the subordinating conjunction. Circle not complex if the sentence is not a complex sentence.

  • Although many people love to eat peanuts, most do not know peanuts are a fruit.
    complex not complex
  • The theater was crowded, and we waited an hour for our tickets.
    complex not complex
  • Before the campers went fishing, they set up their tents.
    complex not complex
  • The puppy cannot be adopted until it has its shots.
    complex not complex
  • Because the students were learning about sea life, they took a class trip to the aquarium.
    complex not complex
  • She enjoys drawing, dancing, and playing soccer.
    complex not complex
  • After bees make honey, beekeepers remove the honey from the beehive with special tools.
    complex not complex
  • We stopped swimming when the lifeguard blew his whistle.
    complex not complex
  • Everyone wants to go down that slide because it is the tallest one in the playground.
    complex not complex
  • Although potatoes and carrots grow underground, their leaves are seen aboveground.
    complex not complex
