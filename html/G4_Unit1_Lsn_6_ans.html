SENTENCES

Lesson 6: Complex Sentences (pp. 28–31)

A complex sentence combines related ideas. The ideas are joined by a subordinating conjunction. The subordinating conjunction may come in the middle or at the beginning of the sentence.

Write a subordinating conjunction from the box to complete each sentence.

after although because before
since until when while
  • Elena ate breakfast she left for class.
  • you were reading, we were playing card games.
  • we ate dinner, we went to the movies.
  • I cannot ride my bike my front tire is flat.
  • Mark knew he would win the race it was his first competition.
  • the pool is closed today, let’s go to the beach.
  • Katharine lost four tennis matches she finally won.
  • we had a great recipe, our pancakes were not good.
  • I always think of summer I hear that song.
  • The runners do not start the race the flag is waved.
Write a complex sentence with the subordinating conjunction while.
