CAPITALIZATION, PUNCTUATION, AND SPELLING

Lesson 47: Quotations (pp. 212–215)

Quotation marks show that a person is speaking. They show a speaker’s or writer’s exact words. Use a comma to separate the speaker’s or writer’s words from other words. Capitalize the first word inside the quotation marks.

Rewrite each sentence correctly. Add the missing comma and quotation marks. Capitalize the first word inside the quotation marks.

  • Marie asked is your family taking a trip this summer?

  • Harry answered we’re going to Canada in August.

  • Selena asked do you want to go to the playground?

  • Dad said please hold this for me for a minute.

  • David exclaimed look at all the toy sailboats on the pond!

  • Ms. Andrews said one hundred people bought tickets to the play

  • Carol asked how much does a ham sandwich cost?

  • Ben said that is my favorite painting in the show.

  • Judy said my aunt opened her own store last week.

  • Larry said my sister left for camp yesterday morning.
