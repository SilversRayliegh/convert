SENTENCES

Lesson 8: Run-on Sentences (pp. 36–39)

A run-on sentence is two complete sentences that run together. You can correct a run-on sentence by writing it as two sentences or by rewriting it as a compound sentence.

Write each run-on sentence correctly. Use and or but if you choose to rewrite the run-on as a compound sentence.

Answers may very. Suggested answers are given.
  • Jasmine had twelve colors of crayons she used only blue and silver.
    Jasmine had twelve colors of crayons, but she used only blue and silver.
  • My brother rushed he was still late.
    My brother rushed, but he was still late.
  • She took the wrong trail she was lost for about an hour.
    She took the wrong trail, and she was lost for about an hour.
  • Peter knocked on my door I kept sleeping.
    Peter knocked on my door, but I kept sleeping.
  • We saw many animals I liked the giraffe best.
    We saw many animals, but I liked the giraffe best.
  • The train went through the mountains we enjoyed the ride.
    The train went through the mountains, and we enjoyed the ride.
  • I had five balloons one balloon broke.
    I had five balloons, but one balloon broke.
  • A bunny hopped across the grass we chased it.
    A bunny hopped across the grass, and we chased it.
  • The pebbles were smooth we filled our pails with them.
    The pebbles were smooth, and we filled our pails with them.
  • My dad mixed the batter he made pancakes for us.
    My dad mixed the batter, and he made pancakes for us.
