Kinds of Sentences
Lesson 27: Contractions with Not (pp. 120–123)

A contraction is a word formed by joining two words, making one shorter word. A contraction is often made up of a verb combined with the word not. An apostrophe (‘) takes the place of any letters that are left out.
Notice how the contractions for cannot and will not are formed. In cannot, both an n and the o are dropped. In will not, the spelling changes.

Read each sentence. Write a contraction for each pair of words in boldface.
  • Our family has not gone to the park once this summer.
  • You should not run with scissors!
  • Jeb and I will not finish the project until Friday.
  • Columbus, of course, had not really reached Asia.
  • I just can not do that many sit-ups.
  • That was not a great horned owl, was it?
  • The smoke alarm did not have working batteries.
  • The weather is not warm enough for softball practice.
  • According to the doctor, Dara does not have the flu.
  • There are not five syllables in foliage
  • You must not feed the elephants at the zoo.
  • Uncle Frank would not have left his house unlocked.
  • The floodwaters have not reached our street yet.
  • Most of the trumpet players were not able to hit the high notes.
  • Poor Jake could not get through the little basement window.
