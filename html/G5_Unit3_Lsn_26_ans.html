Kinds of Sentences
Lesson 26: More Irregular Verbs (pp. 116–119)

Irregular verbs do not add -ed to form the past tense. Their spellings change to form the past tense and the past with has, have, or had.

Write the past form of the verb in parentheses to correctly complete each sentence.
  • The pitcher had thrown a curve ball over home plate. (throw)
  • No one in our class has ridden in a helicopter. (ride)
  • Most of the fifth graders have sung in the choir. (sing)
  • Powerful winds from the west blew through town last night. (blow)
  • Our team last year knew those defensive plays. (know)
  • Dr. Davis spoke to our class about careers in health care. (speak)
  • The old iron gate had broken years ago. (break)
  • For centuries, the church bells have rung every Sunday. (ring)
  • A great many apples fell to the ground. (fall)
  • I had worn a heavy sweater that chilly morning. (wear)
  • The president has spoken to the reporters at the White House. (speak)
  • Several eagles have flown over our farm. (fly)
  • Davy threw the football to Conover. (throw)
  • Someone rode a bicycle down the snowy street. (ride)
  • Our soccer team broke the record for most goals in a game. (break)
