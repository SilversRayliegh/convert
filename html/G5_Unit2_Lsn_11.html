NOUNS

Lesson 11: Singular and Plural Nouns (pp. 52–55)

A noun that names one person, place, thing, or idea is a singular noun.
A noun that names more than one person, place, thing, or idea is a plural noun.
Form the plural of most singular nouns by adding -s. Form the plural of nouns ending in s, ss, x, ch, or sh by adding -es. Form the plural of nouns ending with a vowel and y by adding -s. Form the plurals of nouns ending with a consonant and y by changing the y to i and adding -es.
