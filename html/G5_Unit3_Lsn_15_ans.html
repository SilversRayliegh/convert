VERBS

Lesson 16: Direct Objects (pp. 76–79)

A direct object is a noun or pronoun that receives the action of the verb. It appears in the predicate part of the sentence. A direct object answers the questions what or whom after an action verb.

Draw one line under the verb. Draw two lines under the direct object. If there is no direct object, write no direct object

  • Mud splattered the windshield.
  • The players rushed the goalie.
  • Allison designed the cover herself.
  • The department store plays music on every floor.
  • Did you finish your book report last night?
  • Jana practices early in the morning.
  • Darryl and I telephoned Aunt Anita in Cincinnati.
  • Jaclyn took the keys from her purse.
  • A good architect designed this house.
  • The band plays really well!
  • I loved that delicious pie.
  • Liz shouted loudly down the stairs.
  • Rod fixed the monitor for my computer.
  • Please buy some stamps, Robert.
  • Nat ordered a small pizza with pepperoni.
