Kinds of Sentences
Lesson 15: Action Verbs (pp. 72–75)

The verb is the main word in the predicate. Most verbs are action verbs. An action verb tells what the subject does or did. Action verbs also tell about actions that you cannot see.

Draw a line between the subject and the predicate of each sentence. Then write the action verb.

  • Only fifth graders entered the contest.
  • Gerald practices for the concert.
  • The entire class votes on Tuesday.
  • The tomato sauce simmered for hours.
  • Several colorful peacocks grabbed the bread crumbs.
  • A trained seal performs tricks in the pool.
  • Lana and Mae sell vegetables from their garden.
  • The wise old owl shook his head slowly.
  • The bright, crisp leaves fall from the trees.
  • We climbed Mount Washington last July.
  • A thick red carpet covers Lilly’s bedroom floor.
  • The new highway improved transportation throughout the state.
  • Mrs. Grant gave Grace a recipe for chicken soup.
  • Dolphins and porpoises show great intelligence.
  • Sam’s classmates nicknamed him Scooter.
