Possessive Nouns
Lesson 13: Possessive Nouns (pp. 60–63)

A possessive noun is a noun that shows ownership or possession.
To make a singular noun possessive, add an apostrophe and -s.
To make a plural noun that ends in -s possessive, add only an apostrophe.
To make a plural noun that does not end in -s possessive, add an apostrophe and -s.

Write the possessive form of the noun in parentheses to correctly complete each sentence.

  • The coach took the runner's pulse. (runner)
  • Please follow your teachers' advice. (teachers)
  • The actress's smile seems real. (actress)
  • Tess's music collection is huge! (Tess)
  • Can you find the puppy's leash? (puppy)
  • The children's coats are all downstairs. (children)
  • Those boys' appetites were huge! (boys)
  • Our commynity's park has a small swimming pool. (community)
  • Mrs. Thomas'scar is in the driveway. (Mrs. Thomas)
  • The gesse's honking could be heard all over town. (geese)
  • Did you see that wolf's tail? (wolf)
  • Someone borrowed that womans's bicyle. (woman)
  • The men's tools are in the pickup. (men)
  • My goldfish's name is Bertie. (goldfish)
  • The ponies' saddles are in the barn. (ponies)
