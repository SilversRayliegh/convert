NOUNS

Lesson 10: Common and Proper Nouns (pp. 48–51)

A common noun names any person, place, or thing.
A proper noun names a specific person, place, or thing. Proper nouns begin with capital letters.

Read each sentence. Write common if the noun in dark print is a common noun. Write proper if it is a proper noun.

  • The rose is the state flower of New York.
  • The hikers climbed a high mountain in Colorado.
  • George Washington was the first president.
  • The rings around Saturn are made of ice and rock.
  • We visited Yellowstone National Park last month.
  • We saw many buffalo in the park.
  • Silver Lake is my favorite lake for swimming.
  • The family went for a drive last Sunday.
  • He played a violin at the concert in Green Music Hall.
  • We rode in a canoe down the Mill River.
  • The trip to South America was a wonderful adventure.
  • A famous author signed her books at the library.
  • July and August are my favorite months.
  • Babe Ruth was a famous baseball player.
  • This book has interesting facts about the Sahara Desert.
