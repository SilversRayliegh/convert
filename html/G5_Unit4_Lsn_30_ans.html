ADJECTIVES, ADVERBS, CONJUNCTIONS, AND PREPOSITIONS

Lesson 30: Articles and Demonstrative Adjectives (pp. 136–139)

The special adjectives a, an, and the are called articles. A and an refer to any person, place or thing. The refers to a specific personal place, or thing. This, that, these, and those are demonstrative adjectives. A demonstrative adjective tells which one and always comes before a noun.

Write the word in parentheses that correctly completes each sentence.

  • José was wearing an orange T-shirt. (a, an)
  • The best book on that shelf is Treasure Island. (The, A)
  • Tracy made an honest effort to meet the deadline. (a, an)
  • The peaches from that tree are the sweetest. (that, those)
  • Those bands never play in our town. (That, Those)
  • Here is the last piece to the puzzle. (the, a)
  • Those cartoons are your style of drawing, Delores. (That, Those)
  • I’ll have a taco or two for lunch. (a, the)
  • There’s a hole in my bucket. (a, the)
  • Did that man across the street ask any questions? (that, those)
  • These crackers are my all-time favorite! (This, These)
  • I never had an easier assignment in my life. (a, an)
  • Jill was the older of the two sisters. (an, the)
  • All those books were damaged by water. (that, those)
  • You have come to the end of this activity. (this, these)
