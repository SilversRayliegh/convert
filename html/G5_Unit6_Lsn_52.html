Lesson 52: Parts of a Letter (pp. 232–235)

A friendly letter is an informal letter to someone you know well. In a friendly letter, the greeting and closing begin with a capital letter and end with a comma. In the heading, a comma separates the city and state and also the day and the year.

A business letter is a formal letter to someone you do not know well. It has an inside address, which gives the name and address of the organization or person you are writing to. Place the title of the person after the person’s name. Use a colon (:) at the end of the greeting, and end with a formal closing.
