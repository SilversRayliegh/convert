Unit 4

Adjectives, Adverbs, And PrePositions

Lesson 24: Singular and Plural Nouns (pp. 48–51)

An adjective is a word that describes a noun. It can tell what kind, and it can also tell how many. When two or more adjectives describe the same noun, adjectives that describe that number come first.

Underline the adjective in each sentence once, and underline the noun that it describes twice. Then write the adjective on the line.

  • We looked up at the cloudless, blue sky.
  • The books are heavy.
  • There are nine tiny puppies left in the pet store.
  • Few people were on the train.
  • I rested my head on the soft pillow.
  • We stay indoors on rainy days.
  • Alex thinks snakes are scary.
  • Yuri ate two pieces of toast.
  • Everyone wished me a happy birthday.
  • Three students raised their hands.
  • The grass was dotted with yellow dandelions.
  • We bought fresh bread at the bakery.
  • Mom tries to get eight hours of sleep.
  • Crocodiles have pointy teeth.
  • The movie is based on a true story.
