Lesson 49: Quotations (pp. 220–223)

A quotation is a speaker’s or writer’s exact words.

Rewrite each sentence that is incorrectly written. Use quotation marks, capital letters, and punctuation marks correctly. If a sentence is correct as is, write correct.

  • My dog is afraid of thunder, Danny said.
  • Marcy suggested that we could play bingo.
  • What a huge ship that is Beth exclaimed.
  • Max explained that raccoons eat many kinds of food.
  • Don’t forget to set the alarm clock Dad said.
  • Ms. Chen asked if we were ready for the big game.
  • We’re going to the beach this Saturday Gail said.
  • Is there an article about comets in the encyclopedia Jared asked.
  • Francis Scott Key wrote the national anthem Martha said.
  • How is yogurt made Brendan asked.
