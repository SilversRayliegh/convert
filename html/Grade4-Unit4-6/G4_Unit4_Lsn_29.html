Unit 4

Adjectives, Adverbs, And PrePositions

Lesson 29: Comparing with Good and Bad (pp. 132–135)

The adjectives good and bad have special forms for comparing. Use better when comparing two people, places or things. Use best when comparing more than two. Use worse when comparing two people, places, or things. Use worst when comparing more than two.

Write the form of the adjective in parentheses that correctly completes each sentence.

  • A listener does not interrupt a speaker. (good)
  • Why is black the color to wear on a hot, sunny day? (bad)
  • A bat’s eyesight is than an owl’s. (bad)
  • November is a time for planting than October. (bad)
  • You will have the view of the game from these seats. (good)
  • My new idea for a story is than my old one. (good)
  • We need to think of an idea that is than our last one. (good)
  • Watching television all day is a idea. (bad)
  • The song will win first prize. (good)
  • Samantha had the luck of all the players. (bad)
  • What is the pet for people who live in an apartment? (good)
  • This heat wave is the one we have had in fifty years. (bad)
  • Alex’s pitching skills are than they were last year. (good)
  • This book is about the importance of manners. (good)
  • Spot would be a name for a striped cat. (bad)
