PRONOUNS

Lesson 35: Subject Pronouns (pp. 160–163)

A pronoun is a word that takes the place of one or more nouns.
A subject pronoun is used as the subject of a sentence. It tells whom or what the sentence is about. A pronoun’s antecedent is the noun the pronoun refers to.

Read each sentence. Replace the word or words in boldface with a subject pronoun.

  • The students wait outside the room.
  • The post office is on Main Street.
  • Ms. Martinez teaches math.
  • Thomas looked the word up in the dictionary.
  • John Glenn traveled into space at the age of 77.
  • Sunflowers can grow to be eight feet tall.
  • Jane Goodall studied chimps for many years.
  • The message was written in a secret code.
  • Many ants live together in an underground nest.
  • Grandma showed us how to find the Big Dipper.
  • The first five chapters tell about ancient Egypt.
  • Anna’s father took us to the horse show.
  • The bowl is on the table..
  • A compass always points north.
  • The flag’s fifty stars stand for the fifty states.
