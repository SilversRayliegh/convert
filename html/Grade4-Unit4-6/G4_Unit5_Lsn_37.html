PRONOUNS

Lesson 37: Object Pronouns (pp. 168–171)

An object pronoun follows an action verb. It may also follow a word such as to, in, at, of, with, during, or through.

Read each sentence. Replace the word or words in boldface with an object pronoun.

  • Billy made a frame for the picture.
  • Ted wrote a report about Rosa Parks.
  • Lucky followed Eric and me into the yard.
  • The museum exhibit amazed the visitors.
  • Ella shaped the clay into a ball..
  • The author of the book admires the mountain climbers.
  • We recognized Mr. Robinson right away.
  • Some of the students performed a skit.
  • The crossing guard waved to Molly and me.
  • We need to choose one of the topics.
  • I whispered the answer to my little brother.
  • We thanked Aunt Shamika for the gift.
  • The letter was from Uncle Fred..
  • The park ranger identified the plant right away.
  • I worked on the project with Luisa.
