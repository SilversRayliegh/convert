Adjectives, Adverbs, And PrePositions

Lesson 34: Prepositions and Prepositional Phrases (pp. 152–155)

A preposition is a word that shows how a noun or pronoun is connected to some other word in the sentence. A prepositional phrase is a group of words that begins with a preposition and ends with a noun or pronoun.

Underline the prepositional phrase in each sentence. Then write the preposition on the line.

  • The earth travels around the sun.
  • I leaned against the big oak tree.
  • Our team warmed up before the game.
  • The math club meets after school.
  • Under the sink, you will find a box of tools.
  • Do alligators hatch from eggs?
  • Let’s meet at the flagpole after school.
  • Beyond the farm, we could see rocky hills.
  • A dalmatian is a white dog with black spots.
  • Are there any picnic tables by the lake?
  • We drove along the Pacific Coast Highway.
  • A group of lions is called a pride.
  • Carrots are vegetables that grow below the ground.
  • Mom cut the pizza into six pieces.
  • The Bobcats led until the last minute.
