Pronouns

Lesson 39: Possessive Pronouns (pp. 176–179)

A possessive noun shows who or what has something. A possessive pronoun takes the place of a possessive noun. There are two kinds of possessive pronouns. One kind is used before a noun. The other kind is used alone.

Write the possessive pronoun that correctly completes each sentence.

  • Don’t forget to bring your sneakers. (your, yours)
  • The paintbrushes are theirs. (their, theirs)
  • The striped scarf is hers. (her, hers)
  • A cheetah is known for its speed. (it, its)
  • Our classroom had three large windows. (Our, Ours)
  • Which coat is yours? (your, yours)
  • The knitting needles are mine. (my, mine)
  • Her birthday is in June. (Her, Hers)
  • Yours is the most colorful picture of all. (Your, Yours)
  • I will write my question on a piece of paper. (my, mine)
  • The tent next to the pine tree is thirs. (their, theirs)
  • Is this shopping cart ours? (our, ours)
  • I can hear her voice in the next room. (her, hers)
  • Elephants use their trunks for eating and drinking. (their, theirs)
  • Mine was the longest story of all. (My, Mine)
