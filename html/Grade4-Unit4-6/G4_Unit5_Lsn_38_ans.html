Unit 5

Pronouns

Lesson 38: Using I and Me (pp. 172–175)

The pronoun I is a subject pronoun. I is used only as the subject of a sentence. The pronoun me is an object pronoun. Me is used after an action verb or after a word such as at, for, of, to, or with.

Write I or me to correctly complete each sentence.

  • My family and I traveled to Chicago by train.
  • This game belongs to Ellen and me.
  • The shooting star amazed my friends and me.
  • Kim and I chose a radio station.
  • Ganesh and I are looking forward to the camping trip.
  • My classmates and I made a list of safety rules.
  • Angela showed Bobby and me her new watch.
  • Nick taught Pam and me a new dance step.
  • Cathy and I signed the get-well card.
  • My cousins and I looked through the photo album.
  • Susan gave Tom and me a shell for our collection.
  • Dragana and I learned how to make disappearing ink.
  • My friends and I played tug-of-war.
  • My dad sent Herman and me a book of puzzles.
  • Mom reminded Chris and me about the change to daylight savings time.
