Adjectives, Adverbs, And PrePositions

Lesson 27: Comparing with Adjectives (pp. 124–127)

Adjectives can compare people, places, and things. Adjectives that compare tell how things are different from each other.

Write the form of the adjective in parentheses that correctly completes each sentence.

  • The river in the world is the Nile River. (long)
  • Calm water is for rowing than rough water. (safe)
  • This roof is than that one. (flat)
  • Mornings are the time of all at our house. (busy)
  • Are some of the world’s oceans than others? (salty)
  • We put the shoes in order from smallest to . (large)
  • The gray yarn is than the red yarn. (thin)
  • Cheetahs are the runners in the animal world. (fast)
  • I will take some books out of this box to make it . (light)
  • Tom chose the shirt in his closet. (loose)
  • Which of these chili peppers tastes the ? (hot)
  • Naomi won a prize for growing the pumpkin of all. (big)
  • Apollo is than any other horse. (brave)
  • Misty is the cat I have ever seen.
  • Weston is the member of our family. (young)
