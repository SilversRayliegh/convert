PRONOUNS

Lesson 36: Pronoun-Verb Agreement (pp. 164–167)

In every sentence, the verb must agree with the subject.

Write the present tense of the verb in parentheses to correctly complete each sentence.

  • It faster than any other bird. (fly, flies)
  • He snowflakes in his hands. (catch, catches)
  • They the sandwiches for later. (save, saves)
  • You the envelope. (open, opens)
  • She the map before the trip. (study, studies)
  • I the batter for the muffins. (mix, mixes)
  • We on the door. (knock, knocks)
  • He in a quiet voice. (reply, replies)
  • It under our feet. (crunch, crunches)
  • You the flowers in the vase. (arrange, arranges)
  • She the bow to the package. (attach, attaches)
  • We into the pile of leaves. (jump, jumps)
  • I about the invention. (wonder, wonders)
  • They each key in the lock. (try, tries)
  • It all over the sidewalk. (splash, splashes)
