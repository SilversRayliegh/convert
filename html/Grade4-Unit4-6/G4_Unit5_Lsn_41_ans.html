Pronouns

Lesson 41: Contractions with Pronouns (pp. 184–187)

A contraction is made by joining two words together. An apostrophe (’) takes the place of any letters that are left out. Many contractions are formed by joining together a pronoun and a verb.

Read each sentence. Replace the pronoun and verb in boldface with a contraction.

  • It has been a close game so far.
  • We knew we had met her somewhere before.
  • I am ready for the test.
  • You are not afraid of horses.
  • We will plant vegetables in the garden.
  • They have finished dinner.
  • You have ridden that bus many times.
  • I hope she is coming to the party.
  • They will walk the dog for us this morning.
  • They are the newest members of our team.
  • It is too late to go to the store.
  • I will be Rhonda’s partner.
  • They were glad they had decided to leave early.
  • He is our basketball coach.
  • I wish I had brought an umbrella.
