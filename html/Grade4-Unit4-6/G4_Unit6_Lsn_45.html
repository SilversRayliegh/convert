Unit 6

Capitalization, Punctuation, and Spelling

Lesson 45: Titles (pp. 204–207)

Titles of books, magazines, and newspapers are set off by italics in printed material.
When you write by hand, underline the titles.
Titles of songs and most poems are set off by quotation marks.

Write the title in each sentence on the line. Capitalize each title. Also underline or add quotation marks to set off the title.

  • We sang along as the band played it’s a grand old flag.
  • Did the author of nate the great write any other books?
  • Our library has kids discover and many other magazines.
  • freckle juice by Judy Blume is one of my favorite books.
  • I will now read a poem called the naming of cats.
  • puzzles and games is Steven’s favorite magazine.
  • I borrowed the summer of the swans from the library.
  • Please put that copy of family fun back with the other magazines.
  • message from a caterpillar is a very short poem.
  • Together, we sang this land is your land.
