Unit 4

Adjectives, Adverbs, And PrePositions

Lesson 25: A, An, The (pp. 116–119)

The special adjectives a, an, and the are called articles. These small words come before nouns.

Write the article in parentheses that correctly completes each sentence.

  • That apartment building was once factory. (a, an)
  • Would you like a grapefruit or orange? (a, an)
  • Lake Erie is one of Great Lakes. (a, the)
  • Milo’s aunt is artist. (a, an)
  • elephant can live as long as 70 years. (A, An)
  • Jan looks outside to check weather. (a, the)
  • Does this book have index? (a, an)
  • Our group made model of a volcano. (a, an)
  • The child chose one of toys. (a, the)
  • We saw octopus at the aquarium. (a, an)
  • The crows sat on branch. (a, an)
  • What will cars be like in future? (a, the)
  • The students plan experiment. (a, an)
  • Our boots left footprints in snow. (a, the)
  • Do any people live at South Pole? (a, the)
