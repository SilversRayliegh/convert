Lesson 46: Commas in a Series (pp. 208–211)

A comma separates words or ideas in a sentence and tells the reader when to pause. Sometimes a sentence has a series, or list, of three or more items. Use a comma to separate the items in a series. Do not use a comma after the last word in the series.

Underline the items in a series in each sentence. Then add commas to separate the items.
  • The maple leaves were red orange and yellow.
  • That store sells books magazines and newspapers.
  • Tomorrow will be a cold windy and clear day.
  • Let’s clean sort and stack the boxes.
  • You can dry your hat gloves and shoes by the radiator.
  • Grandpa grows tomatoes lettuce and peppers in his garden.
  • We will feed brush and walk the dog later this afternoon.
  • The mechanics at that garage inspect repair and rebuild old cars.
  • Can you find a penny that is bright shiny and new?
  • Jim Anna and Marcos will meet us at the museum.
  • The king in the story was wise fair and kind.
  • Please help me wash chop and cook the vegetables.
  • My aunt has sheep horses and cows on her farm.
  • That cactus is covered with needles that are long thin and sharp.
  • The three planets closest to the sun are Mercury Venus and Earth.
