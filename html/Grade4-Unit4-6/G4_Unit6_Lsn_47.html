Lesson 47: More Commas (pp. 212–215)

Use a comma to set off an introductory word such as yes, no, or well from the rest of a sentence. Use a comma or commas to set off the name of a person being spoken to.

In each sentence, add commas where they are needed.

  • Michael is this your backpack?
  • Did you know Ruth that a dolphin is really a kind of whale?
  • No I can’t come to the meeting this afternoon.
  • We’ll wait for you outside Paul.
  • Well I’m surprised that it didn’t rain.
  • Can I borrow your pen Louise?
  • I see Tony that you got a new bike.
  • It’s almost time to leave Lori.
  • No I don’t have any overdue library books.
  • Hmm it looks like we’re about to lose the game.
  • Yes I’d like some ice in my water.
  • Are you ready Scott to start rowing?
  • We’re proud of you Felix.
  • Well it’s been a wonderful weekend.
  • Don’t forget to fasten your seat belt Pasha.
