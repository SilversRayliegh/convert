Unit 4

Adjectives, Adverbs, And PrePositions

Lesson 26: Demonstrative Adjectives (pp. 120–123)

Adjectives can tell what kind or how many. Adjectives can also tell which one. Adjectives that tell which one are called demonstrative adjectives. This, that, these, and those are demonstrative adjectives.

Write the demonstrative adjective in parentheses that correctly completes each sentence.

  • We can plant these seeds in the garden. (this, these)
  • Can you read that sign up ahead? (this, that)
  • Wipe your feet on this mat. (this, these)
  • The dance studio is at the end of that hallway. (that, those)
  • Follow these directions to make a pinwheel. (this, these)
  • That parking lot across the street is full. (This, That)
  • Those pots were made by an artist in Mexico. (That, Those)
  • We can play on that basketball court. (those, that)
  • Let’s move these chairs to a shady spot. (that, these)
  • Don’t touch that hot pan. (that, these)
  • Which of these colors do you like best? (these, this)
  • That noise is coming from far away. (That, Those)
  • These coins in my hand are from China. (These, Those)
  • Those houses are on the other side of the lake. (These, Those)
  • Mom will add those walnuts to the salad. (this, those)
