Adjectives, Adverbs, And PrePositions

Lesson 32: Using Good and Well (pp. 144–147)

Good is an adjective that describes a noun. Well is usually an adverb that describes a verb. Well is an adjective only when it refers to someone’s health. When well refers to health, it describes a noun.
