Lesson 7: Compound Sentences(pp. 32-35)

A simple sentence has one subject and one predicate. It expresses one complete idea.
A compound sentence combines two simple sentences that have related ideas.
A conjunction (and, but, or or) joins the two sentences. Always use a comma before the conjunction in a compound sentence.

Read each incomplete compound sentence. Underline the sentence below that best relates to it. Then combine the related sentence to form a compound sentence. Write the compound sentence on the line.

  • Get to the theater by eight, or you will miss the start of the show.
    You will miss the start of the show.
