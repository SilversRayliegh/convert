SENTENCES

Lesson 6: Compound Sentences (pp. 28–31)

Two short sentences with related ideas can be combined to form a compound sentence.
The coordinating conjunction and or but can be used to join the sentences.

Underline the two related sentences in each compound sentence. If the sentence is not a compound sentence, write not a compound on the line.

  • The people on the boat waved to us, and we waved back.
  • I wanted to see the new movie, but all the tickets were sold out.
  • I put the quarter in my pocket, but my pocket had a hole in it.
  • She painted a picture for the contest, and she won a prize.
  • I love shells, and I collect them.
  • I drank all the orange juice in the pitcher.
    not a compound
  • She wrote the message in big red letters, but he didn’t see it.
  • The man and his daughter had a lot of fun at the park.
    not a compound
  • The boy glided over the ice on his skates.
    not a compound
  • The driver honked his horn loudly, but the dog stayed in the road.
