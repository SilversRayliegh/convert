ADJECTIVES AND ADVERBS

Lesson 31: Adjectives (pp. 140–143)

An adverb is a word that generally describes, or tells about, a verb. Adverbs tell how, when, or where an action happens.

Write the adverb and the verb it describes on the lines.

Adverb Verb
  • My friend calmly called for help.
  • We will hang the newest picture there.
  • We drove downtown to the library.
  • Kwame answered most of the questions correctly.
  • I started my art project today.
  • My dad went somewhere with my uncle.
  • The dog waited eagerly for dad’s return.
  • Mike often rides his bike to that store.
  • They went directly to the house from the station.
  • Joan immediately returned the call.
  • Jim hung upside-down from the monkey bars.
  • Bob joined the others later.
  • The answer appeared on the screen instantly.
  • The man performed magnificently in the play.
  • My sister raced upstairs with the news.
