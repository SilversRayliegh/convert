Lesson 47: Capitalizing Proper Nouns and Adjectives (pp. 212–215)

A proper noun names a specific person, place, or thing. Capitalize all the important words in a proper noun. Capitalize proper adjectives formed from proper nouns. If a proper adjective is two words, capitalize both words.

Rewrite each sentence. Capitalize each proper noun or proper adjective correctly
  • Is jackie robinson a member of the baseball hall of fame?
    • Is Jackie Robinson a member of the Baseball Hall of Fame?
  • We drove from toronto to detroit on the freeway.
    • We drove from Toronto to Detroit on the freeway.
  • The arlington memorial bridge crosses the potomac river.
    • The Arlington Memorial Bridge crosses the Potomac River.
  • That armenian restaurant is one block north of first avenue.
    • That Armenian restaurant is one block north of First Avenue.
  • During our visit to the gulf of mexico, we enjoyed some cajun food.
    • During our visit to the Gulf of Mexico, we enjoyed some Cajun food.
  • Did the british army win the battle of bunker hill?
    • Did the British army win the Battle of Bunker Hill?
  • My sister’s Italian class visited the museum of modern art.
    • My sister’s Italian class visited the Museum of Modern Art.
  • That german shepherd on adams avenue is still barking.
    • That German shepherd on Adams Avenue is still barking.
  • A french architect designed washington, d.c.
    • A French architect designed Washington, D.C.
  • Our spanish club viewed two south american movies.
    • Our Spanish club viewed two South American movies.
