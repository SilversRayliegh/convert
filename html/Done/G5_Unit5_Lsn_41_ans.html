PRONOUNS

Lesson 41: Object Pronouns (pp. 184–187)

A subject pronountakes the place of a noun or nouns in the subject of a sentence. An object pronoun takes the place of a noun that is a direct object or the object of a preposition. An object pronoun follows an action verb.

Write the pronoun in parentheses to correctly complete each sentence.

  • Kayla and I work together well. (I, me)
  • Christy and she are coming over to study. (her, she)
  • Does this copy belong to him? (he, him)
  • Mr. Fujita gave the job to them. (them, they)
  • Mrs. White saw Jared and me at the library. (me, I)
  • Did Aunt Gish get a good picture of us? (us, we)
  • They and the others don’t play by the rules. (Them, They)
  • They all went to Detroit with Mr. Mason and her. (she, her)
  • The boys and we played tennis at the park. (we, us)
  • Will you share the money with Gira and me? (me, I)
  • Tad showed Rick and us the new puppy. (we, us)
  • Harry and she have a big problem now! (she, her)
  • Who gave Marsha and him that information? (him, he)
  • Tommy and he will bring the fishing license. (him, he)
  • We saw you and them at the pool. (they, them)
