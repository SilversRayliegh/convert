NOUNS

Lesson 11: Nouns in the Subject (pp. 52–55)

The subject of a sentence tells whom or what the sentence is about. A noun is often the main word in a subject.

Draw a line between the subject and predicate of each sentence. Then write the noun in the subject.

  • The green bowl is full of tulips.
  • The last act is the best in the show.
  • The surprised girl accepted the award.
  • Her proud parents clapped for her.
  • One new store sells sports equipment.
  • All the bears ate berries from those bushes.
  • Only twenty fans watched the game.
  • Many students tried out for the play.
  • Four ducks floated on the pond.
  • My oldest brother won the game.
  • Five paint tubes were left on the table.
  • A red bird landed on that branch.
  • Most shells are white on this beach.
  • My uncle scrambled some eggs for us.
  • All the children chased the fireflies.
