Lessson 6: Compound Predicates (pp. 28–31)

A compound predicate has two or more simple predicates that share the same subject. The predicates are joined by the conjunction and or or.

In each sentence, underline the simple predicates in the compound predicate. Then write the conjunction that joins the simple predicates.

  • The people watched and waited.
  • The satellite gathers and records information.
  • They bought or rented the cabin.
  • The flames blazed and crackled in the fireplace.
  • The robot beeped and buzzed at us.
  • We talked and laughed about our adventures.
  • Either Eliza will agree or disagree with your plan.
  • All of my friends swim and play tennis at the town park.
  • Thunder rumbled, roared and echoed in the valley.
  • My friend Vikram builds and flies model airplanes.
  • These sandwiches look and taste great!
  • Naiman sold or traded the tickets to Junior.
  • My brother and I washed and waxed the old sports car.
  • They either jump or dive into the pool.
  • We read and discuss the stories every day.
