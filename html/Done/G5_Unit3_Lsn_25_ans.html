VERBS

Lesson 25: Irregular Verbs (pp. 112–115)

Regular verbs form the past tense by adding -ed. Irregular verbs do not add -ed to form the past tense. Their spellings change in the past tense and in the past with has, have, or had.

Write the past form of the verb in parentheses to correctly complete each sentence.
  • The party began at about seven o’clock. (begin)
  • Who brought these posters to class yesterday? (bring)
  • Iva has chosen a topic for her report. (choose)
  • Last summer, Zev swam across the lake. (swim)
  • My parents have grown some prize-winning watermelons. (grow)
  • You have taken the wrong jacket from the closet. (take)
  • The baseball flew over the centerfield fence. (fly)
  • Joy and Kana have gone back to the picnic tables. (go)
  • Which poet has written this wonderful poem? (write)
  • Ethan grew three inches last year! (grow)
  • Your kindness has made everyone very happy. (make)
  • Several eagles have flown over our farm. (fly)
  • You have swum over two miles already. (swim)
  • I made this sculpture from an old tricycle. (make)
  • During yesterday’s fire, Captain Graye took charge of the trucks. (take)
