SENTENCES

Lessson 3: Simple Subjects (pp. 16–19)

The simple subject is the most important word in the complete subject. The simple subject tells exactly whom or what the sentence is about.

Underline the complete predicate in each sentence once. Then underline the simple predicate a second time, and write it on the line.

  • Yellow daffodils bloom in the spring.
  • The fierce wind shook the trees.
  • Three tall ships sailed into the harbor.
  • The moon is very bright this evening.
  • The proud team led the parade.
  • Many people jog in the park.
  • A busy, little squirrel ran up and down the tree.
  • Those mittens are too big for me.
  • The tiny baby slept peacefully.
  • A big, red ball rolled past our feet.
  • Most houseplants need plenty of sunlight.
  • The eager volunteers raised their hands.
  • Those yardsticks are thirty-six inches long.
  • The blue water sparkled in the sun.
  • The young children laughed at the silly jokes.
