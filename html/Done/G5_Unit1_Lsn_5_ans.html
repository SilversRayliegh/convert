Lessson 5: Compound Subjects (pp. 24–27)

A compound subject has two or more simple subjects that share the same predicate.
A conjunction is a word that joins words or groups of words. In a compound subject, the conjunction and or or joins the subjects.

  • Radishes and carrots grow underground.
  • Did Denzel or Ali make the carrot cake?
  • My knees and ankles ached after the long hike.
  • The loud thunder and bright lightning scared the baby.
  • Did Sonia or Mai get the lead role in the play?
  • Three monkeys and their trainer juggled oranges.
  • The artist or his helper painted the king.
  • A famous poet and a well-known reporter visited our school.
  • Mr. Davis or his brother worked at the bakery.
  • Fresh air, clean water, and good food are important for health.
  • sheepdog and twenty sheep climbed to the hilltop.
  • My many aunts and uncles made the party lively.
  • Your parent or guardian must sign the form.
  • Tasha and I tied the laces on our ice skates.
  • A tall pine or spruce grew next to the back door.
