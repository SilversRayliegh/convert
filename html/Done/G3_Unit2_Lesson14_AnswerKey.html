NOUNS

Lesson 14: Possessive Nouns (pp. 64–67)

A possessive noun shows who or what owns or has something.
To make a singular noun possessive, add an apostrophe and an -s.
To make a plural noun that ends in -s possessive, add only an apostrophe.

Write the possessive form of the noun in ( ) that correctly completes each sentence.

  • The farmers’ crops grow well in this weather. (farmers)
  • My doll’s arm was cracked. (doll)
  • The photographer’s camera was ready. (photographer)
  • The pitcher’s throw was fast. (pitcher)
  • The girls’ bedroom was sunny. (girls)
  • Our neighbor’s garden is very colorful. (neighbor)
  • The towns’ mayors gave short speeches. (towns)
  • The countries’ leaders worked well together. (countries)
  • The artist’s paintings were beautiful. (artist)
  • Jack carried the boys’ heavy packs. (boys)
  • The players’ shouts got louder and louder. (players)
  • The painter’s work is almost finished. (painter)
  • We followed the animal’s footprints. (animal)
  • The class listened to the teacher’s directions. (teacher)
  • The detective’s face showed his surprise. (detective)
