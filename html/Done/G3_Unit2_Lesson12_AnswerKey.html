NOUNS

Lesson 12: Singular and Plural Nouns (pp. 56–59)

A singular noun names one person, place, or thing.
A plural noun names more than one person, place, or thing. Nouns that end in s, ch, sh, or x form the plural by adding -es. Nouns that end in a consonant and y form the plural by changing the y to i and adding -es.

Write the noun in ( ) that correctly completes each sentence.

  • There are many beautiful beaches in Puerto Rico. (beach, beaches)
  • We finished the job in ten minutes. (minute, minutes)
  • My aunt gave the baby a big kiss. (kiss, kisses)
  • I lost a button from my coat. (button, buttons)
  • We looked in two different dictionaries. (dictionary, dictionaries)
  • Mom bought tickets to a circus. (circus, circuses)
  • We carried many bags from the store. (bag, bags)
  • There are several ranches in the valley. (ranch, ranches)
  • He used a worm as bait. (worm, worms)
  • We told the children not to play with matches. (match, matches)
  • We each ate two bananas . (banana, bananas)
  • The pet store has five cute puppies. (puppy, puppies)
  • What a mess my sister made! (mess, messes)
  • My hobby is photography. (hobby, hobbies)
  • I have three scratches on my arm. (scratch, scratches)
