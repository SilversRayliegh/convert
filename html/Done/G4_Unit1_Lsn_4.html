Lessson 4: Simple Predicates (pp. 20–23)

The simple predicate is the most important word in the complete predicate. The simple predicate tells exactly what the subject does or is.

Underline the complete predicate in each sentence once. Then underline the simple predicate a second time, and write it on the line.

  • We see many kinds of vegetables at the market.
  • The runners train for the race.
  • The ants return to their nest.
  • The weather changed quite suddenly.
  • Brian visited his relatives in Louisiana.
  • Bats are mammals.
  • All rivers flow toward the sea.
  • The caterpillar crawls on the leaf.
  • Maya carries her guitar in a case.
  • Those scientists study the world’s rainforests.
  • Our school year starts in September.
  • Gary’s mother collects unusual rocks.
  • That telescope is very powerful.
  • Penguins waddle across the ice.
  • The telephone rings loudly.
