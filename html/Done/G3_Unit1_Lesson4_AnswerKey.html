SENTENCES

Lesson 4:
Simple Sentences: Subjects (pp. 20–23)

The subject is the part of a sentence that tells whom or what the sentence is about.

Write the subject on the line that best completes the sentence.
  • Many people came to the school play.
    Many people
    Two colors
  • My friend joined our picnic.
    Shiny dimes
    My friend
  • A big crowd watched the football game.
    A big crowd
    A heavy book
  • Swans floated on the lake.
    Rocks
    Swans
  • Loud helicopters whirled above us.
    Loud helicopters
    Two cars
  • Some cows munched grass on the hill.
    Three cartons
    Some cows
  • The children read stories at the library.
    The horses
    The children
  • Two trains pulled into the station.
    Seven worms
    Two trains
  • The performers sang songs on the stage.
    Some rabbits
    The performers
  • Many stars lit up the night sky.
    Many stars
    The rain
