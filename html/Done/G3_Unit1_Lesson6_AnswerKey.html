SENTENCES

Lesson 6: Complex Sentences (pp. 28–31)

A complex sentence combines related ideas. The ideas are joined by a subordinating conjunction. The subordinating conjunction may come in the middle or at the beginning of the sentence.

Write a subordinating conjunction from the box to complete each sentence.

after although because before
since until when while
  • Elena ate breakfast before she left for class.
  • While you were reading, we were playing card games.
  • After we ate dinner, we went to the movies.
  • I cannot ride my bike because my front tire is flat.
  • Mark knew he would win the race although it was his first competition.
  • Since the pool is closed today, let’s go to the beach.
  • Katharine lost four tennis matches before she finally won.
  • Although we had a great recipe, our pancakes were not good.
  • I always think of summer when I hear that song.
  • The runners do not start the race until the flag is waved.
Write a complex sentence with the subordinating conjunction while.
Answers will vary. If the sentence begins with a subordinating conjunction, a comma should follow the first idea.
