ADJECTIVES, ADVERDS, CONJUNCTIONS, AND PREPOSITIONS

Lessson 37: Three Kinds of Conjunctions (pp. 164–167)

There are three kinds of conjunctions. The coordinating conjunctions and, but, and or join two simple sentences to form a compound sentence. The conjunction and also joins simple subjects and simple predicates to form compound subjects and compound predicates. Subordinating conjunctions such as before, because, and when connect related ideas in complex sentences. Correlative conjunctions are pairs of conjunctions that work together to connect two items in a sentence. Both/and, either/or, neither/nor, and not only/but also are correlative conjunctions

Underline the conjunction(s) in each sentence.

  • All scouts must report at the gate before the parade begins.
  • Bananas are a healthy snack, but I do not like them.
  • Neither the washer nor the dryer is working.
  • I introduced myself because I wasn’t sure if she remembered me.
  • Both the poodle and the collie will stay in a kennel.
  • Watching the fireworks from the boat was enjoyable and exciting.
  • When you visit your grandfather, please give him this gift.
  • Not only did we forget our tickets, but also our cash.
  • The children could take swimming lessons or attend basketball camp.
  • The sun beat down on the painted porch, and it started to look dull.
  • The winner is either Sam or Ella.
  • They were walking quickly because they were late.
  • Either I will see you after school or I will see you at the dance.
  • He returned the book, but the pages were torn.
  • Neither the hat nor the kerchief looked quite right.
