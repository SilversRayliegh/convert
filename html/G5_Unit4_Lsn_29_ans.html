ADJECTIVES, ADVERBS, CONJUNCTIONS, AND PREPOSITIONS

Lesson 29: Adjectives (pp. 132–135)

An adjective is a word that describes a noun or a pronoun. An adjective can tell what kind or how many.
A proper adjective is an adjective formed from a proper noun. Like a proper noun, a proper adjective begins with a capital letter. Often the spelling changes.

Underline the adjective or adjectives in each sentence once. Underline the noun that the adjective describes twice.

  • The old otter was alone in the pond.
  • Princeton is an eastern college.
  • The playful, young husky pranced ahead.
  • The basement smelled damp.
  • My mother is busy on Thursday.
  • Jenny’s wrong answers surprised us.
  • The book has a red leather cover.
  • The Mexican bell rang loudly in the tower.
  • The tiny, striped kitten chased the bird.
  • The bike in the garage has a flat tire.
  • Colonial costumes were displayed at the mansion.
  • The path ahead of us was smooth and easy.
  • This French cheese tastes delicious.
  • The encyclopedia is more helpful than the almanac.
  • The club has three new members.
