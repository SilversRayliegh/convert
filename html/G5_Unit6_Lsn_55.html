Lesson 55: Words Often Confused (pp. 244–247)

Words that sound alike or are similar in spelling are often confused. To avoid confusion, learn the meaning and spelling of the words. For example, the word accept is often confused with the word except. When you accept something, you take it or receive it. Except means “not including.”
