Lesson 46: Writing Sentences Correctly (pp. 208–211)

When you write, begin every sentence with a capital letter. End every sentence with an end punctuation mark. The end punctuation you use depends on the kind of sentence you write. End a declarative sentence with a period (.). End an interrogative sentence with a question mark (?). End an exclamatory sentence with an exclamation mark (!). End an imperative sentence with a period (.).

Write these sentences correctly. Use capital letters and the correct end punctuation marks.
  • in the woods we found kindling for our campfire
    • In the woods we found kindling for our campfire.
  • does your watch show the correct time
    • Does your watch show the correct time?
  • move to the rear of the bus, please
    • Move to the rear of the bus, please.
  • oh, what great fun scuba diving is
    • Oh, what great fun scuba diving is!
  • have you visited Lake Tahoe it’s a wonderful place
    • Have you visited Lake Tahoe? It’s a wonderful place.
  • cut a green branch and sharpen the end
    • Cut a green branch and sharpen the end.
  • your brother works at the store he is a salesman there
    • Your brother works at the store. He is a salesman there.
  • are winters in Tallahassee warmer than in Dallas
    • Are winters in Tallahassee warmer than in Dallas?
  • do try these doughnuts, guys they really are delicious
    • Do try these doughnuts, guys. They really are delicious!
  • dismount from the parallel bars to your right
    • Dismount from the parallel bars to your right.
