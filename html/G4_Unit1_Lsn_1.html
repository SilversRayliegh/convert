Kinds of Sentences

SENTENCES

Lessson 1: Kinds of Sentences (pp. 8–11)

There are four kinds of sentences.
A declarative sentence makes a statement and ends with a period (.).
An interrogative sentence asks a question and ends with a question mark (?).
An imperative sentence gives a command and ends with a period (.).
An exclamatory sentence shows strong feelings and ends with an exclamation mark (!).

Add the correct end punctuation to each sentence. Then write declarative, interrogative, imperative, or exclamatory to tell what kind of sentence it is.

  • Is this your jacket
  • Read this riddle carefully
  • What a heavy backpack this is
  • What did Grandma say in her e-mail
  • These bananas are not ripe yet
  • Did you memorize the poem
  • Miami is Florida’s largest city
  • Please answer the telephone
  • Wow, that was an exciting game
  • Has anyone seen my math book
  • Put the puzzle pieces back in the box
  • This park has two soccer fields
  • We were so tired by the end of the hike
  • Has today’s mail arrived yet
  • Tell me what you did in school today
