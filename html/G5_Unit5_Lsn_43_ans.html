Lesson 43: Possessive and Demonstrative Pronouns (pp. 192–195)

A possessive pronoun takes the place of a possessive noun. It shows who or what has or owns something. There are two kinds of possessive pronouns. The possessive pronouns my, your, his, its, our, and their appear before a noun. The possessive pronouns mine, yours, his, hers, its, ours, and theirs stand alone. The demonstrative pronouns this, that, these, and those point out a specific person, place, or thing. Use this and these to point out things that are nearby. Use that and those to point out things that are farther away.

Write the possessive or demonstrative pronoun in parentheses that correctly completes each sentence.
  • The mouse lost its way in the maze. (it, its)
  • The photos in the album are ours . (our, ours)
  • This is the story that I wrote today. (This, These)
  • Mrs. Cho signed her name on the painting. (its, her)
  • The idea for the carnival was ours. (our, ours)
  • Have you ever met my cousin Leticia? (my, mine)
  • Theserings on my fingers are solid gold. (These, Those)
  • From this airplane, those houses down there look tiny. (these, those)
  • Mikey says this sweatshirt is his . (him, his)
  • The big white house on the hill is theirs . (their, theirs)
  • How much for that hat on the top shelf way in the back? (this, that)
  • Thisone right here is only $10. (This, That)
  • Ourdinner was overcooked and rather tasteless. (Our, Ours)
  • Here is my coat, and this one must be yours . (your, yours)
  • I’ve never seen those boots before in my life. (that, those)
