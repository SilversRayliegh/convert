ADJECTIVES, ADVERBS, CONJUNCTIONS, AND PREPOSITIONS

Lesson 32: Comparing with Good and Bad (pp. 144–147)

The words better and best are the two forms of the adjective good that are used for comparing. The words worse and worst are the two forms of the adjective bad that are used for comparing. Do not use -er or -est or more or most when comparing good and bad.

Write the form of the adjective in parentheses that correctly completes each sentence.

  • The jazz concert was than the rock concert. (good)
  • The drummer was the musician in the band. (good)
  • Of the four sisters, Rania sings . (good)
  • Sean has a temper than his brother Brian. (bad)
  • This year’s pear harvest is than last year’s. (bad)
  • Of all your jokes, that was clearly the . (bad)
  • My drawing is bad, but yours is . (bad)
  • This is the photo of Mom that I’ve ever seen. (good)
  • Let’s go to a restaurant than this. (good)
  • This week’s weather was the I’ve seen in months. (good)
  • That car will look if you polish it. (good)
  • Michael’s character trait is his sincerity. (good)
  • The part of the climate here is the humidity. (bad)
  • No meal could taste than this one! (good)
  • If anything, the second act was than the first. (bad)
