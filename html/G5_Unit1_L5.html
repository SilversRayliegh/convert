SENTENCES

Lesson 5: Compound Sentences (pp. 24–27)

Sometimes two simple sentences contain related ideas. You can make a compound sentence by combining these simple sentences. Use a coordinating conjunction such as and, but, or or to join the sentences.

Read each incomplete compound sentence. Underline the sentence below that best relates to it. Then combine the related sentences to form a compound sentence. Write the compound sentence on the line.
  • My cousin came to visit, and
    We talked all afternoon.
    Her birthday is in January.
  • We need to leave now, or
    We enjoyed the movie.
    We will be late for the movie.
  • I like books about sports, but
    I like mysteries even better.
    You can find them in the library.
  • Ben’s family brought salad to the picnic, and
    Yolanda’s family brought hot dogs.
    They forgot to bring napkins.
  • You can borrow my umbrella, or
    You can wait inside until the rain stops.
    My raincoat is yellow.
  • Angela was sick yesterday, but
    She sent me a postcard.
    She feels fine today.
  • We can take the bus to the library, or
    We can apply for library cards.
    We can walk.
  • Lightning flashed, and
    Thunder roared.
    I planted flowers.
  • I opened the door, and
    My pen ran out of ink.
    The cat ran out.
  • The pond was frozen, but
    The ice was too thin for skating.
    Ice-skating is a popular sport.
