Lessson 8: Complex Sentences (pp. 36–39)

A complex sentence is made up of two related ideas joined by a subordinating conjunction. The following subordinating conjunctions are often used to connect the related ideas: after, although, because, before, since, until, when, while.

Underline the two related ideas in each complex sentence. Write the subordinating conjunction that joins them.

  • I wake up when the rooster crows.
  • Terri won’t sing because her throat hurts.
  • After Mom leaves work, she usually goes to the gym.
  • This toaster hasn’t worked right since we bought it.
  • Although Dad likes dogs, we can’t have one in our apartment.
  • Before we moved to Ohio, my family lived in Colorado.
  • I can’t go to the movies until I finish my report.
  • After we finished the pizza, we had blackberries for dessert.
  • Aunt Iris knits while the rest of us watch TV.
  • Since winter is coming, I bought a new sweater.
  • We didn’t see the deer, because of the fog.
  • Unless it rains, we’ll go to the beach tomorrow.
  • Please close the door when you leave.
  • Although I like science, math is my favorite subject.
  • Let’s review these skills before we take the test next week.
