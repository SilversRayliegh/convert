VERBS

Lesson 18: Simple-Tense: Past (pp. 84–87)

A past-tense verb tells about an action that already happened. Follow these rules for forming past-tense verbs:

Add -ed to most verbs.
If a verb ends in e, drop the e and add -ed.
If a verb ends in a consonant and y, change the y to i and add -ed.
For most one-syllable verbs that end in one vowel followed by one consonant, double the consonant and add -ed.
Write the past-tense form of the verb in parentheses that correctly completes each sentence.
  • Pirate ships carried gold here long ago. (carry)
  • A terrible storm destroyed our garage last month. (destroy)
  • Mr. Diego searched for his lost wallet at the park. (search)
  • All week, we hoped for better weather. (hope)
  • Lettie just grabbed the last sandwich in the basket. (grab)
  • The students studied hard for the exam. (study)
  • Mom dropped the hot platter in the kitchen. (drop)
  • Grandma married Grandpa a long time ago. (marry)
  • Two snowmobiles zipped across the field. (zip)
  • Every child in the room stared at the new teacher. (stare)
  • We scrubbed the kitchen floor for your visit. (scrub)
  • The dog team traveled over 150 miles. (travel)
  • Our class planned a winter carnival. (plan)
  • The bushes and flowers beautified the schoolyard. (beautify)
  • Native Americans first explored this wilderness. (explore)
